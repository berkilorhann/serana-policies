# Data Deletion Request — Serana

This page explains how to request deletion of your data for the **Serana — AI
Companion** app, developed by **CosmicLoopByte**.

## How to Request Deletion

Serana does not use accounts. Your data is linked only to an anonymous device
ID generated by the app on first launch. To have all data linked to your
device deleted:

1. **Before uninstalling the app**, send an email to
   **cosmicloopbyte@gmail.com** with the subject line **"Data Deletion
   Request — Serana"**.
2. Tell us the approximate date you started using the app and, if possible,
   your display name used in the app. This helps us locate the record tied to
   your device ID.
3. We will confirm your request and delete the data. Verified requests are
   fulfilled within **30 days**, and we will notify you by reply email when
   deletion is complete.

> **Note:** If you uninstall the app first, the anonymous device ID stored on
> your phone is destroyed and we may no longer be able to match you to your
> server-side data. That data becomes orphaned and is removed in periodic
> cleanups, but if you want immediate, confirmed deletion, contact us before
> uninstalling.

## What Is Deleted

When your request is fulfilled, we permanently delete all server-side data
linked to your device ID:

- Profile information (display name, age, bio, chat style preference)
- All chat conversation history with AI characters
- In-app activity (likes/passes, gifts sent, unlocked media)
- Credit balance and entitlement records held on our servers

## What Is Retained

- **Google Play purchase records** are held by Google under its own policies;
  we cannot delete those. Manage them in your Google Play account.
- Anonymized, aggregated statistics that cannot be linked back to any device.
- Records we are legally required to keep (e.g., for tax or fraud-prevention
  purposes), which are retained only as long as the law requires.

There are no other extended retention periods: once a deletion request is
fulfilled, no personal data linked to your device ID remains on our systems.
